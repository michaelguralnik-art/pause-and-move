What does it mean to feel at home in your body?

By Michael Guralnik · March 2025 · 6 min read

In our fast-paced, screen-dominated lives, we tend to live almost entirely in our heads. We treat our bodies like vehicles to transport our minds from one meeting to the next. But truly feeling healthy requires more than just maintaining the vehicle; it requires occupying it fully. It means feeling at home in your body.

Feeling at home in your body is a state of somatic comfort and awareness. It is the ability to perceive your internal state without judgment. It is knowing when you are tired before you collapse, recognizing tension as it starts rather than when it hurts, and moving with a sense of ease and coordination.

When we lack this connection, we experience a sense of somatic alienation. We feel clumsy, perpetually tense, or surprised by sudden physical pain. Somatic movement practices, such as the Feldenkrais method and Connected Movement, focus on rebuilding this connection. They do not force the body into rigid postures; instead, they guide you through small, curious movements to expand your sensory awareness.

By cultivating this quality of attention, you start to reclaim your physical self. You learn how your ribs move as you breathe, how your weight shifts as you walk, and where you hold unnecessary effort. This sensory awareness is the true foundation of lasting wellness and physical freedom.
