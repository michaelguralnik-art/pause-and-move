Healthy living

Creating a daily ritual of mindful breathing and pause

December 2024 · 4 min read

We take around 20,000 breaths every single day, yet we rarely pay attention to a single one. Most of the time, we breathe shallowly into our upper chest, a pattern that signals mild stress to our nervous system. Taking control of your breath is the fastest way to change your physiological state.

Mindful diaphragmatic breathing stimulates the vagus nerve, which acts as the main highway for your parasympathetic ('rest and digest') nervous system. By slowing down your breath and breathing deeply into your abdomen, you send an immediate signal to your brain that you are safe. This lowers your heart rate, reduces blood pressure, and decreases cortisol levels.

Creating a daily breathing ritual does not require hours of meditation. Just five minutes a day can yield profound benefits. Find a comfortable seat, place one hand on your chest and one on your belly. Inhale through your nose for a count of four, letting your belly expand, and exhale slowly through your mouth for a count of six.

This simple pause acts as a reset button for your mind and body. Over time, this practice builds somatic resilience, helping you navigate daily stressors with greater calm and preventing stress from accumulating as physical tension in your body.
